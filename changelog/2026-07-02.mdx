Five new areas shipped this week, extending the [v1.14.x platform](/changelog/2026-07-01) with knowledge management, technician scheduling, a field-tech mobile experience, and the first CRM & sales surfaces.

## New features

### CRM

The system of record for the companies and people you sell to and support. Each client opens to a 360° account view — contacts, tickets, quotes, invoices, RMM agents, contracts, and compliance in one place. Includes a customizable deal pipeline with weighted forecasting (deal value × win-probability), an activity log for calls, meetings, and follow-ups, and email campaigns with open, click, and bounce stats. **AI lead capture** turns a raw sales-call transcript into ready-to-save contact, activity, and deal records — nothing writes until you confirm. See [CRM](/crm/index).

### Discovery blueprint

The bridge from a sales deal to a billable quote. Upload a prospect's discovery materials and Nexie reads them alongside the client's industry profile and your product catalog to produce a **Client Operations Blueprint** — an executive summary, risk score, recommended product/hardware/software/security stack, compliance frameworks, and a suggested contract design. Review, approve, and convert to a **draft quote** in one click. Blueprints are versioned, and each analysis records its estimated AI cost against your usage log. See [Discovery blueprint](/assessment/index).

### Knowledge base

A searchable article library for SOPs, how-tos, and troubleshooting steps — organized by category and tag, scoped to your tenant, and full-text searchable with relevance ranking and highlighted snippets. Draft a first pass with AI from a plain-language prompt, then review and publish. Relevant published articles surface automatically inside a helpdesk ticket. See [Knowledge base](/kb/index).

### Dispatch board

Assign technicians to helpdesk tickets and work orders from a single dispatchable queue. Choose from **Calendar**, **Kanban**, **List**, **Map**, or a full-screen **TV mode** for ops-room displays; each assignment becomes a dispatch slot that moves through Scheduled → Dispatched → En route → On site → Complete. **AI Suggest** recommends the best tech for a job based on skills, proficiency, and current load, and one-way **Microsoft 365 calendar sync** pushes assignments to the assigned tech's Outlook calendar. See [Dispatch board](/dispatch/index).

### Mobile app

A field-tech Progressive Web App at `/m/` — install it from your phone's browser with **Add to Home Screen**. A unified task feed buckets your work into **Urgent / Today / This week**, work timers are server-side and shared with the desktop, and the weekly timesheet can be reviewed and submitted from the phone. See [Mobile app](/mobile/index).

## Known limitations

- **CRM** — campaign **send** records the send and its stats, but outbound email delivery integration is limited today; treat campaigns as planning and tracking until delivery is fully wired. AI lead capture is preview-only, accepts only `.txt`/`.md` uploads (up to 10 MB), and requires a tenant Claude API key.
- **Discovery blueprint** — analysis needs at least one successfully processed document; image-only or unsupported files may extract poorly. Malformed analyses revert the assessment to **Draft** for a re-run. After converting to a quote, review pricing — items the AI couldn't price fall back to your catalog price or zero. Requires a tenant Claude API key.
- **Knowledge base** — no article version history or scheduled publishing yet; edits overwrite in place. View counts are best-effort. AI article drafting requires a tenant Claude API key.
- **Dispatch board** — Microsoft 365 calendar sync is **one-way** (NEXOS CORE → Outlook); changes made in Outlook aren't read back. Map view groups jobs by **city and state**, not precise coordinates. AI Suggest requires a tenant Claude API key.
- **Mobile app** — security-ops actions, project detail, and purchase orders aren't available on mobile yet. Use the desktop app for those.

Earlier [known limitations from v1.14.x](/changelog/2026-07-01#known-limitations) remain unchanged.
